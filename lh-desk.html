Founder & operator life desk
Track 1 · Human capital

If your face is on the deck or your name is on the personal guarantee, you are the risk. The Founder & Operator Life Desk treats your income, health, and survivability as part of the capital stack, not an afterthought.

We work with founders, partners, and key operators to model what happens if you die, get disabled, or step away at the wrong time — and then we fund those scenarios on purpose.

Coverage we typically combine
  • Key person life insurance on owners and core operators.
  • Private long-term disability structured around your real income, not just W-2.
  • Buy-sell funding so partners can buy shares without fire-selling the business.
  • Executive health and gap coverage to keep downtime low and surprises limited.
The goal isn’t “more policies.” It’s making sure a human failure doesn’t trigger covenant breaches, forced sales, or stalled raises.
How we run a founder consult
  • Map your income sources: salary, distributions, bonuses, side deals.
  • Map your obligations: family, debt, investors, personal guarantees.
  • Stress-test: death, disability, extended illness, extended funding winter.
  • Design minimum viable coverage: what absolutely must be funded on Day 1.
  • Layer optional upgrades once the business cash flow can handle it.
Used by other desks

This is also how we prep founders we work with on the AI, solar, and art desks. Human capital coverage is wired in before we scale asset or infrastructure risk.

Middle-class protection desk
Track 2 · Households

The Middle-Class Protection Desk is built for people in motion: young families, first-time homeowners, working couples, and solo earners who don’t have a CFO — but still have a lot to lose.

We optimize for one idea: if something happens, you keep your home, keep your kids on track, and don’t saddle anyone with a surprise.

Core tools we use
  • Level term life sized to income and mortgage balance.
  • Mortgage protection and income gap coverage so one event doesn’t cost the house.
  • Final expense coverage sized to realistic burial/ceremony costs.
  • Cancer, critical illness, and accident plans to catch out-of-pocket shocks.
What we ask in a household consult
  • Who depends on you financially, and for how long?
  • How long do you want the house to be “untouchable”?
  • If your income stopped for 12–24 months, what breaks first?
  • What dollar amount would let your family breathe if the worst happened?
  • What is a monthly number that would feel heavy, but sustainable?
We’d rather build a smaller, durable plan you can actually keep than a flashy one you lapse in year three.
Life & health catalog
Track 3 · Full design

When the situation calls for more than basic protection, we pull from the full life & health catalog. The difference is in how we stack it: each piece has a job inside your overall financial, business, or family plan.

Term life

Clean, temporary coverage matched to specific obligations: mortgages, school years, business debt, or defined income windows.

Often the backbone of middle-class and early-career founder planning.
Whole life / permanent life

Long-horizon coverage with cash value that can be used for liquidity, legacy, or specific strategies if designed correctly.

We only recommend it when the time horizon and cash flow make sense.
Indexed universal life (IUL)

Flexible permanent structures tied to index performance with downside floors. Can be useful for targeted accumulation strategies when risk-tolerances and fees are understood.

Disability income

Protects your ability to earn, which is usually your biggest asset. We look at occupation, income variability, and how long your personal runway really is.

Health & supplemental

Individual and group health structures, plus supplemental layers like accident, hospital indemnity, and critical illness to catch shocks.

Annuities & income products

For clients closer to preservation than growth, we can bring in annuity structures to create predictable, contractual income streams.

How we actually work with you
Process
Step 1
Mapping your real downside

We start with simple, direct questions: who relies on you, what you own, what you owe, and what would break first if your income or health disappeared for a while.

Step 2
Designing a minimum viable plan

We build a “floor” first — the smallest structure that secures the essentials: home, dependents, business continuity. Only then do we layer on optional, more advanced strategies.

Step 3
Coordinating with other advisors

When needed, we sync with your CPA, attorney, or planner so coverage plays nicely with tax, estate, and entity structures.

Step 4
Ongoing check-ins

Life moves. We prefer short annual or milestone check-ins to keep coverage aligned with new jobs, raises, kids, homes, or businesses.

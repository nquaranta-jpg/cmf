Context
Why art needs its own risk language

Art does not behave like ordinary property. Values move with taste, provenance, condition, and markets. Works travel, hang in unpredictable environments, and pass through multiple hands and venues every year.

The Art Risk Desk exists to translate that complexity into clean coverage structures, so curators, lenders, and owners can focus on the work itself instead of worrying about every truck ride, exhibition, and storage unit.

Approach
How we work a collection

1. Map the works

We build a simple picture of the collection: what exists, where it lives, how it moves, and how it is valued. High-level first, detailed scheduling as needed.

2. Match coverage to reality

We compare current policies to how the art actually behaves. That includes transit patterns, loan activity, storage conditions, and any planned exhibitions.

3. Restructure and place

Once gaps and overlaps are clear, we restructure limits, terms, and conditions, then place coverage with markets that understand art risk.

4. Stay with the movement

Collections and programs change. We stay in the loop on major acquisitions, sales, and shows, so coverage keeps up with the work rather than lagging behind it.

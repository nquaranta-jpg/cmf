Problems we get called for
Friction points
“The lender will not close.”

A project is ready on slides, but the lender or IC will not sign off on construction or downtime risk. We design coverage structures that answer specific lender concerns, not vague boilerplate.

“Existing quotes do not match the project.”

Standard property or tech policies are being stretched to fit GPU campuses and mining conversions. We rebuild the submission so markets can see and price the real risk.

“We have stranded power and no strategy.”

Mining sites, power-heavy facilities, or partial builds exist with no clear path to AI. We help frame whether those assets can credibly become compute infrastructure that insurance and capital will support.

How the desk runs a deal
Process
Step 1 · Triage
Is this real and in-scope?

We start with a short conversation and a basic project overview: assets, power profile, counterparties, size, and timing. The goal is to decide quickly whether the project is in-scope and worth taking to market.

  • Screen for seriousness and scale
  • Clarify capital plan and counterparties
  • Align on timelines and constraints
Step 2 · Structure
Map the risk and build the file

We map construction, operational, and downtime risk, identify what matters most to lenders and sponsors, and build a submission markets can actually underwrite.

  • Risk mapping across site, tech, and contracts
  • Draft coverage stack with lines, limits, and conditions
  • Prepare narrative, schedules, and supporting documents
Step 3 · Market & iterate
Take it to markets that know infra

We place coverage through wholesaler and carrier partners, translate their feedback back into the deal, and iterate until the risk, coverage, and capital stack line up.

  • Approach targeted markets, not everyone at once
  • Log declines, indications, and conditions transparently
  • Adjust structures so coverage supports the financing path
Agency, not MGA
Crown Merchant Financial operates as an insurance agency. We do not hold underwriting authority and do not bind coverage on our own paper. Our role is to see the project clearly, prepare the submission well, and guide it through markets that understand power and infrastructure risk. The carrier takes the risk; we manage the relationship, structure, and execution.

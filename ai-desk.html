AI POWER RISK DESK

Insurance built for compute-dense power.

The AI Power Risk Desk focuses on facilities where power, cooling, and uptime are the business: AI data centers, mining-to-AI conversions, and high-density compute campuses that need credible coverage to unlock capital.

Producers ramped through the CMF Academy learn how to speak both insurance and power-infrastructure so they can sit at the table with founders, lenders, and investors — not just vendors.

What this desk actually writes
Desk focus · Account types
Core focus

We focus on power-intensive, compute-dense facilities where insurance is a gating item for financing:

  • AI data centers and GPU campuses
  • Bitcoin mining sites converting to AI compute
  • Hybrid mining + compute operations
  • Existing industrial sites retooled for AI workloads
Lines & structure

The desk coordinates coverage across property, liability, and operational risk so the capital stack makes sense:

  • Property and equipment (including GPU fleets)
  • Business interruption and uptime-linked exposure
  • Environmental / pollution where applicable
  • Liability tied to co-location or hosted compute clients
Ideal accounts

This desk is most effective when the operator is thinking like a capital allocator, not just a miner:

  • Operators planning to refinance or raise institutional capital
  • Sites with long-term power agreements or grid relationships
  • Conversions with a clear AI or HPC customer story
  • Founders who understand the value of clean risk narratives

What a real account flow looks like
Example motion
1. Discovery

Producer runs a structured discovery with the operator and, when possible, the capital partner:

  • Power profile, interconnection, and redundancy
  • Capex and timelines for buildout or conversion
  • Existing coverage, limits, and lender requirements
  • Planned customer mix (AI tenants, self-use, hybrid)
2. Framing the risk

The desk turns raw details into a coherent risk narrative for carriers and capacity providers:

  • Clarify risk controls and uptime strategy
  • Position the site as an infrastructure asset, not a punt
  • Align proposed structure with lender expectations
  • Set realistic expectations on limits and pricing
3. Long-term relationship

The goal is not a one-off policy. It is to be in the room every time the operator raises, refinances, or expands:

  • Annual and mid-cycle reviews tied to capex
  • Supporting coverage for new sites or expansions
  • Helping operators present clean narratives to capital
  • Turning insurance into a strategic advantage

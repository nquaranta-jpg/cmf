For AI power projects, insurance is no longer a box to check at closing — it's a gating input for the entire underwriting memo.

Carriers want to know how the asset behaves across load, redundancy, and time. Lenders want confidence that loss scenarios won't vaporize cash flow or covenants.

We bridge those requirements and help teams articulate a risk story that makes sense from both a technical and financial perspective.

Brief · Power demand

AI power demand & the 2028 crunch

Analysts project a double-digit power shortfall for U.S. data centers by 2028. The bottleneck isn't chips — it's permitted, insured, financed power. Coverage terms are starting to influence where capital will actually build.

Note · Infrastructure reuse

The mining site as a neocloud asset

Sites built for mining already solved land, power, and cooling. With the right risk and credit structure, they become fast-track AI compute campuses — or stranded assets, if no one can underwrite the transition.

Perspective · Energy & capital

Insurance as a gating item for SMRs

Small modular reactors won't scale on engineering alone. Long-dated, credible coverage is critical before serious capital commits. We monitor how carriers are talking about nuclear, cyber, and grid interaction around SMR pilots.

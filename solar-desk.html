SOLAR RISK DESK

Coverage for solar as a real asset, not a science project.

The Solar Risk Desk focuses on utility-scale solar, storage-linked projects, and structured portfolios where coverage is woven into lender and investor requirements — not treated as an afterthought.

Producers from the CMF ramp learn how to talk in the language of project finance, PPAs, and long-term asset performance so they can sit across from sponsors, not just small-ticket buyers.

What this desk actually writes
Desk focus · Account types
Core focus

We concentrate on solar projects with real capital behind them and a clear operating story:

  • Utility-scale solar farms (with or without storage)
  • Commercial and industrial rooftop portfolios
  • Community solar and distributed project roll-ups
  • Solar + storage hybrids tied to PPAs or tariff programs
Lines & structure

The desk helps align coverage with project documents and lender requirements:

  • Construction and operational all-risk property
  • Delay in start-up and business interruption
  • General liability and products/completed operations
  • Optional coverages tied to performance and degradation
Ideal accounts

The best fits are sponsors and developers who are already thinking in portfolios and vintages:

  • Developers with repeat pipelines, not one-offs
  • Funds rolling up operating projects into vehicles
  • Operators building multi-site commercial portfolios
  • Teams who understand that clean coverage helps raise capital
How producers get here from the ramp

Desk standard
You are part producer, part translator.

The Solar Risk Desk needs people who can translate between engineering, legal documents, and carrier appetites. That means you must be disciplined with your notes, precise in your language, and honest about what a policy can and cannot do.

What a real account flow looks like
Example motion
1. Project intake

Producer gathers details from the developer or sponsor and, when possible, the lender:

  • Project size, location, and construction timeline
  • PPA terms or revenue framework
  • EPC and O&M contracts, key counterparties
  • Lender covenants and insurance requirements
2. Structuring coverage

The desk coordinates coverage to match the real risk, not just a template checklist:

  • Align limits and deductibles with lender expectations
  • Clarify construction vs. operational phases
  • Address key exposures like hail, wind, and fire
  • Account for storage where batteries are involved
3. Portfolio relationship

The goal is to grow with the developer or fund across multiple deals:

  • Help standardize coverage across a portfolio
  • Support future acquisitions and refinancings
  • Stay close to changes in regulation and incentives
  • Protect relationships with both carriers and sponsors
Who actually belongs on this desk
Producer fit
Signal we look for
  • Interest in energy markets, renewables, and policy
  • Patience for long sales cycles with multiple parties
  • Ability to manage detail across documents and timelines
  • Respect for the fact that real assets have real consequences
Not required, but helpful

Any prior exposure to solar development, construction, or project finance is helpful — but the real filter is whether you can stay organized, think clearly, and communicate in a way that lenders and underwriters actually trust.
